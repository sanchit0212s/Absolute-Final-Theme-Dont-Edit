## Theme 4 — Prān
**Directory**: `theme-pran/`
**Port**: 5177
**Status**: Complete demo ✅

**Visual identity**
- Palette: Near-monochrome — warm paper + deep ink + ONE accent: terracotta clay
- Mood: Editorial magazine / documentary — not a store, a studio visit. Confident, restrained, intelligent.
- Layout: Typography-first viewport hero · Intention-based navigation (Explore/Discover/Workshop/Connect) · Horizontal lookbook scroll · Photo essay sections · Full-screen Discovery experience
- Type: Playfair Display (display) + Karla (body) — completely different pairing from all other themes
- Distinctive: No hero image (just text), intention cards replace both hero CTA and guide, "Voices" not "Testimonials", "Bring Home" not "Add to Cart", horizontal lookbook browsing, near-monochrome with single terracotta accent, editorial whitespace as luxury

**Key innovations**
- The homepage IS the brand story — products are discovered through narrative, not grids
- "The Discovery" replaces the quiz with three full-screen questions presented as a revelation, not a wizard
- Intention-based homepage prompt ("What are you making space for?") embeds guide logic directly into the landing experience
- URL structure itself signals brand intent: `/forms`, `/form/:handle`, `/discover`, `/workshop`, `/connect`

## Theme 5 — Āgama
**Directory**: `theme-agama/`
**Port**: 5178
**Status**: Complete demo ✅

**This is not a theme. It's a different business architecture.**

- Palette: Gallery white + ink black + ONE accent: brass gold (#B8860B). No warmth, no gradients. Clinical like a museum.
- Type: Space Grotesk (display — geometric, tech-modern) + Inter (body). Zero serifs.
- Navigation: Floating overlay menu, no traditional navbar. Just "DIVINE ARTS · RESERVE · MENU".

**Structural innovations (not cosmetic)**
- **Question-first landing**: Homepage opens with "Why are you here?" — three paths for three visitor types (sacred space / gift / curious). No hero, no banner, no images.
- **Single-product focus**: After choosing a path, the ENTIRE homepage becomes about ONE recommended deity. Like Apple launching iPhone.
- **Weight as hero metric**: "1.8 kg" is the headline, not the price. Because weight is what separates real brass from Amazon hollow junk.
- **"Reserve" not "Add to Cart"**: Made-to-order framing. "Reserve This Form" + "Complete Reservation."
- **WhatsApp as primary CTA**: Green WhatsApp button appears alongside every action. That's how ₹18K purchases actually happen in India.
- **Consecration as calendar event**: "Next ceremony: April 3 · 4 of 8 spots open." Visual spot indicator. Creates urgency.
- **Artisan attribution**: Every product shows "Cast by Shri Gopal Vishwakarma · 24th year at the workshop." Like a wine label.
- **Customer homes gallery**: "Where our forms live" — real homes, real altars, real cities.
- **Weight comparison section**: "What 1.8 kg feels like" — relatable comparisons (3 books, a water bottle).
- **3 pages, not 6**: Home (the experience) / Forms (the catalog) / Story (the workshop). Stripped to essentials.
